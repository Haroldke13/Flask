Latest developments in climate change

Carbon Footprint Calculator is an application that helps individuals calculate their carbon footprint and find offset projects. As of 2023, the global carbon emissions have increased by 1.5% compared to 2022. The world is facing a climate crisis, and it is crucial that we take action to reduce our carbon footprint. Here are some recent updates in the climate change battle:

COP26

The Conference of the Parties (COP26) took place in Glasgow, UK, from 26 September to 10 October 2022. It was a crucial meeting where countries came together to discuss the Paris Agreement and the roadmap for achieving net-zero emissions. The talks focused on the need for urgent action to address climate change and the importance of reducing carbon emissions. The COP26 resulted in the signing of the Glasgow Common Statement, which outlines a series of actions countries must take to strengthen their climate commitments.

COP27

The second Conference of the Parties (COP27) was held in Marrakech, Morocco, from 7 November to 12 December 2018. This meeting built on the success of COP21 and further emphasized the need for countries to take bold action to address climate change. The Marrakech Accord adopted by the Conference represents a significant step forward in the fight against climate change. It sets out a roadmap for countries to progressively reduce their greenhouse gas emissions and achieve net-zero emissions by 2050.

The Paris Agreement
